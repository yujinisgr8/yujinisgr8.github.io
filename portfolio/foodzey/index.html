Foodzey

Foodzey Product Documentation

Overview

This project aims to help people reduce food waste by tracking daily grocery purchase and food consumption. We designed a system to inspire, guide, and assist users develop better habits to reduce food waste.

My Role

I am a project leader and product designer in a team of four. Other team members are Rahul Sawn, Yueran Zhao, and Jonathan Timma. We were all master student in School of Information, University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI. This project last for 4 months from Winter to Spring in 2017. I encouraged the whole team to dive into the topic of food waste initially, and worked with rest of us to closely as a product designer. I designed the prototype with the team and refined it on my own after the project was done.

The Challenge

Many people have experienced the shortcomings of improperly planned food management. We sometimes buy extra groceries, to minimize the number of shopping trips we have to make. However, this can lead to wastage of food if the groceries aren’t consumed in a timely fashion. In addition to wasting food, this also ends up making us spend more money on food than we actually need to. The wastage of food also have negative impact on environment. Food that is produced but not eaten is responsible for adding 3.3 billion tonnes of greenhouse gases to the planet’s atmosphere (FAO, Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, 2013).

Goals

Our team wanted to design a system that helps people better manage their food purchase and consumption in order to reduce food waste and carbon dioxide emission.

Approaches

We reached out to literature and people to understand causes and effects about wastage of food. We did experiments on ourself in order to scope the complex of tracking daily food purchase and consumption, find out user pain points and desire, and explore what can we help.

Research & Interview — The Discovery

Research

Forty percent of the food in the United States is never eaten. But at the same time, one in eight Americans struggles to put enough food on the table. —Natural Resource Defense Council

People have been working hard on solving this problems, and we noticed a lot of different solutions. An organization reFED reported 27 solutions to food waste, and we found our niche in technology for better food tracking and analytics.

We looked through FAO’s comprehensive research about food waste, research has shown that food waste happens all along the supply chain. Since we want to focus on tackling the problem from the consumer end for better purchase and consumption management, and in developed countries consumers are the greatest contributors to food waste, we found causes from consumption are

  1. Lack of awareness and education about ways to save money and prevent wasted food
  2. Packaging sizes and designs
  3. Retail management to track shelf-life
  4. etc.

Our team is designing a personal informatics product, so we ask ourself how can we arise people’s awareness, and educate people to reduce food waste. So we reached out to people and asked about their current food management strategy, frustration, and desire.

Interview

Scope the Goal and the Design Framework

Target User We decided to target on young professionals because we found out this community has strong needs to track and manage food consumption in order to control finance expense and contribute to the environment. Goal We want to enhance the experience of measuring and tracking food purchase and consumption, and motivate young professionals to reduce food waste by giving financial guidance in food purchase and emotion inspiration. Framework for design Hence, our design will focus on measurement strategies on food waste, and intervention to help users reduce food waste.
